About Alex Settle, Esq.

South Jersey Roots

Alex was born and raised in Egg Harbor Township, a larger community in Atlantic County, New Jersey, where he graduated from Egg Harbor Township High School in 2006. He earned his undergraduate degree from Montclair State University in 2010 and went on to earn his law degree from Touro Law School in 2012.

After law school, Alex began his legal career in private practice. He worked alongside a solo practitioner and later opened his own law office, representing clients in a wide range of criminal cases. These early experiences taught him the importance of listening closely to clients, building trust, and preparing meticulously for trial.

Alex later joined the Cape May County Prosecutor’s Office, where he served as an Assistant Prosecutor on a trial team and supervised the Gangs, Guns, and Narcotics Task Force. In that role, he handled complex, high-stakes cases involving violent crimes, firearms, and large-scale drug operations. His time as a prosecutor gave him firsthand knowledge of how criminal cases are investigated, charged, and tried—insight that now gives him a strategic edge as a defense attorney.

He then joined the Public Defender's Office in Atlantic County. He has tried numerous cases to verdict and is known for his aggressive advocacy, relentless preparation, and deep commitment to justice. Alex currently serves as the Deputy First Assistant Public Defender in the Atlantic County Trial Region.

In recognition of his trial experience and legal expertise, Alex is certified by the New Jersey Supreme Court as a Criminal Trial Attorney—a designation awarded to only a select group of attorneys who demonstrate substantial experience and proficiency in criminal law.

Educator & Advocate

Alex also serves as an adjunct professor at Rowan University, where he teaches Law & Human Rights, Law & Society, and Criminal Law. His courses help students understand how laws are shaped by society—and how they, in turn, shape lives.
